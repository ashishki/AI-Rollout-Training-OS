# Packaging And Pricing

Version: 1.0
Last updated: 2026-05-21
Owner: GTM lead

AI Rollout Training OS is packaged around safe role-specific AI adoption, not
generic prompt training. Pricing should scale with active learners, role packs,
governance depth, integrations, and deployment model. Do not promise guaranteed
productivity gains; use pilot outcome metrics and customer-provided assumptions.

## Buyer And Value Metric

| Field | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| Primary buyer | VP Support, Enablement, RevOps, AI Transformation lead, or operations executive accountable for safe AI rollout. |
| Economic buyer trigger | Need to prove safe AI adoption, reduce unmanaged AI use, satisfy governance review, or scale approved workflows across teams. |
| Value metric | Active learners in launched cohorts, with expansion drivers for role packs, governance exports, integrations, and deployment isolation. |
| Packaging boundary | Training workflows, policy-grounded feedback, manager approval, reporting, governance evidence, and reliability/security operations. |

## Pricing Rules

- Use active learners as the primary recurring pricing unit.
- Add pricing bands for role-pack count, cohort volume, integration count,
  governance export frequency, sampling volume, and deployment isolation.
- Treat SAML, private networking, customer-managed keys, custom data residency,
  and bespoke control frameworks as scoped add-ons until proven repeatable.
- Use the ROI calculator only with customer-provided assumptions and measured
  pilot metrics.
- Do not discount by removing safety-critical features such as manager approval,
  policy approval, audit logging, or sensitive-data controls.

## Sales Qualification

Strong fit:
- Named buyer owns AI rollout, enablement, support operations, RevOps, or AI
  governance.
- Pilot success can be measured through activation, completion, approved
  workflow changes, risk rate, manager review SLA, and expansion intent.
- Security or governance review is a reason to buy rather than only a blocker.

Weak fit:
- Buyer only wants generic prompt classes.
- No manager or operator can review outputs.
- Customer demands unsupervised autonomous workflow changes.
- Procurement requires custom regulated deployment before pilot value is proven.

## One-Page Sales Summary

AI Rollout Training OS helps teams launch role-specific AI training with policy
grounding, guardrails, manager approval, and governance evidence. The commercial
path starts with a Team Pilot, expands through Enterprise Enablement, adds
Governance Plus when auditability is the buyer's priority, and uses Regulated
Single-Tenant only when deployment isolation is required.
